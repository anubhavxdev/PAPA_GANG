Water Saving Tips

Personalized recommendations to help you conserve water efficiently

Personalized for Your Home

Based on your usage patterns and devices, these tips can help you save water and money:

Reduce Shower Time

Cutting your shower time by just 2 minutes can save up to 1,750 gallons of water per person annually. Try setting a timer or playing a 5-minute song as a guide.

Potential Savings: 1,750 gallons/year per person

Full Loads Only

Your laundry usage is 20% higher than average. Only run your washing machine with full loads to maximize efficiency. This can save up to 15-45 gallons per load.

Potential Savings: 15-45 gallons per load

Fix That Leak

Our system detected a potential small leak in your kitchen faucet. A faucet that drips once per second can waste more than 3,000 gallons per year.

Potential Savings: 3,000+ gallons/year

Tips by Category

Check for Toilet Leaks

Add a few drops of food coloring to your toilet tank. If the color appears in the bowl without flushing, you have a leak that's wasting up to 200 gallons per day.

Potential Savings: Up to 200 gallons/day

Turn Off While Brushing

Turn off the water while brushing your teeth or shaving. This simple habit can save more than 200 gallons per month for a family of four.

Potential Savings: 200+ gallons/month

Refrigerate Drinking Water

Instead of running tap water until it's cold, keep a pitcher of water in the refrigerator. This eliminates the waste of running water that occurs while waiting for it to cool.

Potential Savings: Up to 1 gallon per drink

Efficient Dishwashing

When washing dishes by hand, don't let the water run. Fill one basin with wash water and the other with rinse water. This can save up to 10 gallons per washing session.

Potential Savings: 10 gallons per washing session

Choose Water-Wise Plants

Select native plants that are adapted to your local climate. These typically require less water and maintenance than non-native varieties.

Potential Savings: Up to 550 gallons/week

Water in the Morning

Water your garden in the early morning when temperatures are cooler to minimize evaporation. Avoid watering during windy weather.

Potential Savings: 25-50% of outdoor water

Apply Mulch

Add a layer of mulch around plants to reduce evaporation, inhibit weed growth, and improve soil health. Use organic materials like wood chips or compost.

Potential Savings: 20-30% of garden water

Install a Rain Barrel

Collect rainwater from your roof in a rain barrel for watering your garden. A 55-gallon drum can fill up during just a quarter-inch rainfall on an average-sized roof.

Potential Savings: 1,300 gallons during rainy season

Install Low-Flow Showerheads

Replace standard showerheads with WaterSense labeled models, which use less than 2 gallons per minute while maintaining good water pressure.

Potential Savings: 2,700 gallons/year per showerhead

Upgrade to High-Efficiency Toilets

Replace older toilets with WaterSense labeled high-efficiency models that use 1.28 gallons per flush or less, saving thousands of gallons annually.

Potential Savings: Up to 13,000 gallons/year

Energy Star Appliances

When replacing washing machines or dishwashers, choose ENERGY STAR certified models, which typically use 33% less water than standard models.

Potential Savings: 3,000 gallons/year

Faucet Aerators

Install aerators on kitchen and bathroom faucets. These inexpensive devices mix air with water to maintain pressure while reducing flow to 1.5 gallons per minute or less.

Potential Savings: 700 gallons/year per faucet

Shorter Showers

Try to keep showers under 5 minutes. Use a shower timer or play a 5-minute song to help you track time and make it fun.

Potential Savings: 12.5 gallons per shower

Sweep, Don't Spray

Use a broom instead of a hose to clean driveways, sidewalks, and patios. A hose can waste 100 gallons in just minutes.

Potential Savings: 100 gallons per cleaning

Car Washing

Use a commercial car wash that recycles water instead of washing your car at home. If you do wash at home, use a bucket and sponge with a hose nozzle that can be turned off.

Potential Savings: Up to 100 gallons per wash

Capture Cold Water

While waiting for hot water to reach the faucet or shower, collect the cold water in a bucket and use it for plants or cleaning.

Potential Savings: 2-3 gallons per day

Water Conservation Facts

The Average Home

The average American home uses about 88 gallons of water per day per person, with up to 50% used outdoors.

Small Changes Matter

If all U.S. households installed water-efficient fixtures, water use would decrease by 30%, saving 5.4 billion gallons per day.

Global Impact

By 2025, two-thirds of the world's population may face water shortages. Conservation is increasingly critical.
